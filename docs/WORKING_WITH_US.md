# Working With Us

This challenge is intentionally open-ended. Asking focused questions is part of the work, not a sign of failure.

You may contact the project team to clarify context, test assumptions, request missing information, or pressure-test a direction.

## Required interaction points

### 1. Kickoff note

Send this within your first 45–60 minutes:

- your interpretation of the task;
- two or three directions you considered;
- what you plan to investigate first;
- the largest unknown that could change your approach.

### 2. Direction check

Before substantial coding, send:

- selected use case;
- selected model and dataset;
- proposed test and metric;
- key assumption;
- main risk;
- any decision you need confirmed.

### 3. Final risk check

Before finalizing the report, send:

- current conclusion;
- confidence level;
- biggest limitation;
- any unanswered question that could materially change your recommendation.

## How to ask for help

A useful request contains:

1. The decision you are trying to make.
2. The options you considered.
3. Your current hypothesis or recommendation.
4. The specific information you need.
5. Whether the issue blocks progress.

Example:

> I am choosing between a commercially relevant claim-verification task and a cleaner academic classification benchmark. I currently recommend claim verification because it better tests evidence use, but the public datasets are weaker. Should commercial relevance outweigh benchmark cleanliness for this exercise? This does not block my model-accessibility research.

Avoid messages that only say “I am stuck” or ask the team to perform research you can reasonably do yourself.

## Working efficiently

- Bundle related questions.
- Keep an assumptions log.
- Continue independent work while awaiting non-blocking answers.
- Surface failed tests and weak evidence rather than silently changing the story.
- You may request up to two short synchronous conversations; asynchronous interaction is preferred.
